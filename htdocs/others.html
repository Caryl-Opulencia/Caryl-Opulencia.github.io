How do society view intelligence?

As time passes by, the society's view on intelligence is starting to develop, improve, and change for the better. However, many people still do think that only people who excel in school, good in math and sciences, and fluent in englsih are the only ones to be considered as intelligent. We all have to understand that the concept of intelligence is broad and is actually vague. Found below are the words used by several people to describe others whom they consider beyond average in terms of intelligence. (Tap on any word to know meaning.)

Takeaways

  • Know that you are different from anyone else in the world.
  • Smart shaming or Anti-intellectualism has become a part of Filipino culture and it is TOXIC.
  • Stop invalidating other people's opinions just because you felt they were way better than you.
  • Be more self-aware and at the same time considerate of other people.
  • You are what you think of most of the time.
  • You are loved; you are enough; and you are valid; I am proud of you! I believe in you!

Intelligent
- the general term used in descrbing peoples having or indicating a high or satisfactory degree of intelligence and mental capacity; revealing or reflecting good judgment or sound thought(Merriam Webster, 2020).

Genius
- a person who displays exceptional intellectual ability, creative productivity, universality in genres or originality, typically to a degree that is associated with the achievement of new advances in a domain of knowledge.

Smart
- having or showing a high degree of mental ability : most commonly associated with intelligent and bright.

Creative
- having or showing an ability to make new things or think of new ideas. : using the ability to make or think of new things : involving the process by which new ideas, stories, etc., are created. usually disapproving : done in an unusual and often dishonest way.

Innovative
- for its part, can refer to something new or to a change made to an existing product, idea, or field. (of a product, idea, etc.) featuring new methods; advanced and original.

Intellectual
- relating to the intellect or its use. b : a person who developed or chiefly guided by the intellect rather than by emotion or experience : rational. c : requiring use of the intellect intellectual games.

Gifted
- having exceptional talent or natural ability. Someone who is gifted has a natural ability to do something well. As if being born with such exceptional ability

Wise
- having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment. Can respond sensibly or shrewdly to a particular situation having knowledge in a specified subject..

Street-Smart
- being street smart means you have a good environmental or situational understanding. You know what's going on around you. More importantly, you can see what's happening around you. You are able to make judgments on the scenario, the place, and people around you, and you are able to trust these personal judgments.

Clever
- quick to understand, learn, and devise or apply ideas, skilled at doing or achieving something; talented.

Skillful
- having the training, knowledge, and experience that is needed to do something well : having a lot of skill. : showing or requiring skill.

Curious
- interested in learning about people or things around. eager to know or learn something. Arousing attention or interest because unusual or strange; one of the scientific abilities (ie. curiosity).

Talented
- having a natural aptitude or skill for something. A special ability that allows someone to do something well. common talents include: dancing, singing, acting, playing sports etc.

Know-it-all
- a person who acts as though he or she knows everything and who dismisses the opinions, comments, or suggestions of others. He/she has a general background or info to various fields.

Athletic
- active or gifted in sports, games, or exercises . physically strong, fit, and active. It could refer ro someone or something that is well-inclined in sports and consider it as there field of experty.

Poet
- a person who writes poems. a person possessing special powers of imagination or expression. one who writes poetry: a maker of verses. one (such as a creative artist) of great imaginative and expressive capabilities and special sensitivity to the medium.

Imaginative
- involving new, different, or exciting ideas; involving the use of your imagination. His/her span of thinking is beyond others.

Empathic
- showing an ability to understand and share the feelings of another.

Nerd
- engage in or discuss a technical field obsessively or with great attention to detail. a foolish or contemptible person who lacks social skills or is boringly studious.

Book-worm
- a person who likes to read books and who spends a lot of time reading and studying.

Rational
- comes from the Latin word rationalis, meaning reasonable or logical. If you're rational, you do things based on logic, as opposed to impulse or whimsy. The original meaning in English was of something endowed with the ability to reason.

Jack of all Trades
- often a compliment for a person who is good at fixing things, and has a very good broad knowledge. a person who can do many different types of work.

Dilligent
- having or showing care and conscientiousness in one's work or duties.

Bright
- has a vivid understanding about certain issues.

Studious
- spending a lot of time studying or reading. done deliberately or with a purpose in mind. showing great care or attention.

Fast-learner
- A fast learner is someone who embodies the skills of being a strategic thinker and a good listener and applies them to learning quickly. A strategic thinker can come up with several solutions to solve a problem and will analyze them quickly to determine the best course of action.

Flexible
- able to be easily modified to respond to altered circumstances or conditions. capable of bending easily without breaking. ready and able to change so as to adapt to different circumstances.

Multi-intelligent
- someone that has a great ability in learning and acquiring information ranging from the using of words, numbers, pictures etc.

Adaptive
- describe people who don't lose their cool when plans change quickly and they are always willing to learn new ways to do things. Being adaptive helps you sail along in today's ever-changing world.

Responsible
- having an obligation to do something, or having control over or care for someone, as part of one's job or role.

Music
- vocal or instrumental sounds (or both) combined in such a way as to produce beauty of form, harmony, and expression of emotion.

Keen
- commonly means eager, interested, or enthusiastic. This sense of the word is often followed by about or on and the particular interest, as in He's very keen on music. More generally, keen means sharp, intense, or strong. It can be used to mean sharp in a literal way, as in a keen blade.

Sharp Memory
- If you describe someone as sharp, you are praising them because they are quick to notice, hear, understand, or react to things. By memory, it means that person has a high memory capacity and is able to retain informations even after a long period of time.

Witted
- having wit or understanding —usually used in combination dull-wittedquick-witted. quick in perception and understanding: mentally alert.

Inventive
- having the ability to create or design new things or to think originally. showing creativity or original thought.

Optimistic
- disposed to take a favorable view of events or conditions and to expect the most favorable outcome. feeling or showing hope for the future.

Sanaol
- SANA LAHAT DI BA?

Leader
- someone who can see how things can be improved and who rallies people to move toward that better vision. Leaders can work toward making their vision a reality while putting people first. Just being able to motivate people isn't enough — leaders need to be empathetic and connect with people to be successful.

IQ
- stands for intelligence quotient. IQ tests are tools to measure intellectual abilities and potential. They're designed to reflect a wide range of cognitive skills, such as reasoning, logic, and problem-solving.

SQ
- Ability to build a network of friends and maintain it over a long period of time.

EQ
- Emotional intelligence (otherwise known as emotional quotient or EQ) is the ability to understand, use, and manage your own emotions in positive ways to relieve stress, communicate effectively, empathize with others, overcome challenges and defuse conflict.

AQ
- AQ stands for adversity quotient, or simply put, resilience.

Brilliant
- distinguished by unusual mental keenness or alertness. Performs very good and excellent.

Most of the words listed above are society's connotation when talking about people who seem to be special because of their minds. But what the society fails to understand is that we are all special in our own ways. We are capable of varying things and blessed with different gifts. We should stop invalidating other people's capacities just because they do not seem to fit our bullshit toxic standards. Why bullshit and toxic? Because some people end up not knowing where to place themselves because when you make mistakes in grammar or you can't solve a simple polynomial function, you will be labelled as 'bobo', 'mangmang', and 'hindi nag-iisip'. BUT when you corrected someone for their mistakes, or you share some facts and information, you'll be attacked by lines: "EDI WOW", "ikaw na magaling", "akala mo kung sino", "oo na ako na bobo", "pabibo". It really just makes no sense at all like as if it was the person's fault that he is thinking, reading, studying, and sharing helpful facts.

Smart Shaming
culture

- also known as Anti-intellectualism is an act of mocking someone who is deemed to be more intellectual than others and insulting them for their display of intellect. These are common responses of Filipinos, yes mostly Filipinos, (because here in the Philippines, being intelligent is as if a curse) to insightful ideas, facts, and knowledge that they don't have any knowledge about. Really, what is to wrong about knowing things? Why is there a need to be ashamed of? Living in a society with the culture of ignorance and hostility to the intellect is just so toxic and problematic. Let us all just stop and accept the fact that we don't know everything and we shall be open for oppurtunities to grow and learn.

Mental Health Issues

  • Lowered Self-Esteem

    Due to societal's standards, people mostly students tend to have lower confidence and belief in themselves. They would usually compare themselves with other people who seem to be more fit to the society and would up feeling down. They would doubt themselves several times because other people keep on invalidating their talents, telling they are failures and would succeed if they pursue their passion. They would question themselves thinking they are not enough, and they are stupid.

  • Bullying

    This could happen both to people who are intelligent in terms of the current societal's standards and to those who are labelled as stupid. In a classroom setting, students would be afraid of asking and answering questions for fear of making mistakes, being embarassed, and being laugh at. Most of the time, other students would laugh and bully the other one who made mistake and couldn't answer a simple question. In the other hand, bullying could also be in the form of smart shaming. They will be called as nerd, book-worm, pabibo, and the likes. In simpler words, bullying happens due to the belief that being different is abnormal when in fact we are all different from each other.

  • Insecurities

    Insecurity is a tendency of a person to lack confidence or certainty in oneself, may be experienced by most people regarding some aspect of their lives (goodteraphy.org). As discussed above, insecurity is brought by lack of confidence and comparison with others. This happen when a person is not contented with himself for the society is telling him that he is not enough and he should be this that.

  • Depression

    Depression (major depressive disorder) is a common and serious medical illness(psychiatry.org) that causes a persistent felling of sadness and loss of interest(mayoclinic.org). This is actually a medical disorder which people do not usually understand and pay attention to. It is due to several factors and some of which are found above (lowered self-esteem, bullying, and insecurities). Patients tend to degrade their self-worth thinking that they are no longer worthy of living.
